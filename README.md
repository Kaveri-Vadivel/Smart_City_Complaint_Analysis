# Smart_City_Complaint_Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes a Smart City Complaint dataset to identify complaint patterns, service inefficiencies, complaint hotspots, and factors affecting citizen satisfaction. The analysis focuses on complaint categories, department performance, resolution delays, geographic distribution, and service quality to generate actionable insights and recommendations for city authorities.

---

## Objective

The main objectives of this project are:

* Identify recurring civic issues across city zones.
* Analyze complaint categories and resolution patterns.
* Evaluate department performance and resolution efficiency.
* Measure the impact of resolution delays on citizen satisfaction.
* Develop interactive dashboards for data-driven decision-making.
* Generate recommendations to improve urban service delivery.

## Key Findings

* Traffic Signal Malfunction was the most frequently reported complaint category.
* South Zone recorded the highest complaint volume.
* Water Board and Traffic departments showed the highest resolution delays.
* Resolution delay significantly impacts citizen satisfaction.
* Only 27.32% of complaints were marked as resolved.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Improve response times for high-priority complaints.
* Allocate additional resources to overloaded departments.
* Focus preventive maintenance efforts on recurring complaint categories.
* Monitor complaint hotspots for proactive intervention.
* Continuously track citizen satisfaction and service performance.
